What to Expect in Your First Month on GLP-1

Starting a new medication can be daunting. Here's a week-by-week guide to what you might experience when beginning Wegovy or Mounjaro treatment.

15 Jan 2025 5 min read

The First 30 Days: Your Body's Transition

The first month on GLP-1 medications like Wegovy or Mounjaro marks a significant transition as your body adjusts to the medication. During this time, the medication begins to mimic natural hormones that regulate hunger and blood sugar, and you may notice changes in your appetite and digestion almost immediately.

Understanding what's normal during this adjustment period can help you feel more confident and prepared for your weight loss journey.

Week-by-Week Timeline

Week 1: Initial Adjustment

During your first week, you'll start at the lowest dose to minimise side effects. Most people begin noticing a subtle decrease in appetite within the first few days. You might find yourself:

  • Feeling fuller faster during meals
  • Having less interest in snacking between meals
  • Experiencing mild nausea, particularly after eating
  • Noticing changes in bowel movements

Week 1 Tip: Start keeping a food and symptoms journal. This will help you identify patterns and triggers, making it easier to manage side effects as you continue treatment. The GLPer app makes this simple with built-in food diary and side effects logging features designed specifically for GLP-1 patients.

Week 2-3: Body Adaptation

As your body continues to adjust, you may experience more noticeable appetite suppression. Common experiences during this period include:

  • Reduced cravings for high-fat and sugary foods
  • Feeling satisfied with smaller portion sizes
  • Mild digestive discomfort that typically improves
  • Initial weight loss becoming visible (typically 2-5 pounds)

If you do experience side effects, they are most likely to occur during the first few weeks of treatment. The most common include nausea, digestive discomfort, and temporary fatigue as your body processes food more slowly and you adjust to a lower caloric intake.

Week 4: Finding Your Rhythm

By the end of your first month, most patients have adapted to the starting dose. You should be noticing:

  • More consistent energy levels throughout the day
  • Better understanding of which foods work best for you
  • Established eating patterns that support your treatment
  • Measurable weight loss progress (typically 5-10 pounds total) — track your progress with the GLPer app's weight logging feature

At this point, you may be preparing for your first dose increase. Remember that when your dose increases, you may experience a temporary return of mild side effects as your body adjusts to the higher dose.

Expected Weight Loss Results

Clinical studies show impressive first-month results:

  • Mounjaro: Most people lose approximately 4% of body weight in the first month at the starting dose, with studies showing up to 22.5% total weight loss when combined with diet and exercise
  • Wegovy: Average weight loss of 5-10 pounds in the first month, with clinical trials showing up to 17.5% total weight loss at the 2.4mg maintenance dose

Individual results vary based on diet, activity level, starting weight, and how your body responds to the medication. The key is consistency and patience as you build sustainable habits.

Common Side Effects Timeline

Side effects typically occur within the first few weeks of starting the medication and tend to subside over time. The most common side effects include:

Most Frequent Side Effects:

  • Nausea: The most commonly reported symptom, often accompanied by reduced appetite
  • Digestive changes: May experience either constipation or diarrhoea as gut motility adjusts
  • Fatigue: Temporary tiredness as your body adapts to lower caloric intake
  • Bloating: Feelings of fullness that typically improve within 2-4 weeks

These side effects are usually mild to moderate and improve significantly within 2-4 weeks as your body adjusts. When your dose increases after 4 weeks, you may experience a brief return of symptoms that typically resolve quickly.

Tips for a Smooth First Month

Managing Nausea

  • Eat smaller, more frequent meals throughout the day
  • Avoid fatty, greasy, or highly spicy foods
  • Stay well-hydrated with water or sugar-free beverages
  • Take your injection at a consistent time (many find evening works best)
  • Don't lie down immediately after eating

Optimising Your Diet

  • Focus on lean proteins to maintain muscle mass
  • Choose whole grains and fibre-rich foods to support digestion
  • Drink 8-10 glasses of water daily (GLP-1s can reduce thirst sensation)
  • Avoid alcohol, which can worsen side effects
  • Identify your personal food triggers through journaling

Building Sustainable Habits

  • Establish a regular meal schedule, even if portions are smaller
  • Incorporate gentle physical activity as energy allows
  • Track your progress with photos and measurements, not just the scale — the GLPer app helps you monitor weight, medications, meals, and side effects all in one place
  • Join a support group or connect with others on the same journey
  • Maintain regular check-ins with your pharmacist or healthcare provider

When to Contact Your Pharmacist

While most side effects are normal and temporary, contact Logan Health Pharmacy if you experience:

  • Severe or persistent vomiting that prevents you from staying hydrated
  • Severe abdominal pain that doesn't improve
  • Signs of dehydration (dark urine, dizziness, dry mouth)
  • Allergic reactions (rash, swelling, difficulty breathing)
  • Side effects that significantly impact your daily life

Remember: You're Not Alone

The first month is a learning experience. Most people find that side effects improve significantly by week 3-4, and the benefits of reduced appetite and steady weight loss make the initial adjustment period worthwhile. Our team at Logan Health is here to support you every step of the way.

Looking Ahead: Months 2-5

After your first month, you'll gradually increase your dose under pharmacist supervision. Each dose increase helps you achieve better results while allowing your body to adapt gradually:

  • Month 2: First dose increase, continued weight loss acceleration
  • Month 3: Further dose titration, side effects typically minimal
  • Month 4-5: Reaching maintenance dose, establishing long-term habits

The gradual dosing schedule is designed to maximise effectiveness while minimising side effects, giving you the best possible experience on your weight loss journey.
