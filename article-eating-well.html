Eating Well on GLP-1: Foods That Support Your Treatment

Maximise your results with the right nutrition. Learn which foods work best with GLP-1 medications and how to manage reduced appetite effectively.

10 Jan 2025 7 min read

Nutrition: The Foundation of Your Success

When taking GLP-1 medications like Wegovy or Mounjaro, your appetite naturally decreases, and you'll find yourself eating less. This makes every bite count. The best diet to follow while on GLP-1 medications is one that focuses on whole, minimally processed foods that deliver maximum nutrition in smaller portions.

When your appetite decreases, what you eat becomes even more important. Eating fewer foods means there's an increased probability you won't get in as many nutrients as you need. That's why nutrient density matters more than ever.

Key Principle:

If you're taking in fewer calories because you're on a GLP-1, those calories need to deliver in terms of protein, fibre, and essential nutrients. Focus on quality over quantity.

Priority Nutrients for GLP-1 Patients

1. Protein: Your Most Important Nutrient

Protein is crucial when taking GLP-1 medications because it helps preserve muscle mass during weight loss, keeps you feeling satisfied, and supports overall health.

Daily Target: Aim for approximately 1.2 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight (or 0.55 grams per pound). For a 150-pound person, that's about 82 grams daily.

Best Protein Sources:

  • Lean meats: Chicken breast, turkey, lean beef
  • Fish: Salmon, cod, tuna, sardines (omega-3 bonus)
  • Eggs: Versatile and easily digestible
  • Dairy: Greek yoghurt, cottage cheese, skyr
  • Plant proteins: Tofu, tempeh, edamame, lentils, chickpeas
  • Protein supplements: Protein shakes or powders when whole foods aren't appealing

Pro Tip: Include protein at every meal and snack. It helps stabilise blood sugar and keeps you satisfied longer.

2. Fibre: For Digestion and Satiety

Fibre is an important nutrient that helps you feel full, stabilises blood glucose, and prevents constipation (a common side effect of GLP-1 medications).

High-Fibre Foods to Prioritise:

  • Whole grains: Oats, quinoa, brown rice, whole wheat bread
  • Vegetables: Leafy greens, broccoli, Brussels sprouts, carrots
  • Fruits: Berries, apples, pears, oranges
  • Legumes: Lentils, black beans, kidney beans, chickpeas
  • Seeds: Chia seeds, flaxseeds, psyllium husk

Target: Aim for 25-30 grams of fibre daily, increasing gradually to avoid digestive discomfort.

3. Healthy Fats: Don't Skip Them

Healthy fats are rich in monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fats, which actually help stimulate the release of natural GLP-1 in your body. They're also essential for absorbing fat-soluble vitamins.

Best Sources of Healthy Fats:

  • Avocados: Rich in monounsaturated fats and fibre
  • Nuts and seeds: Almonds, walnuts, chia seeds, flaxseeds
  • Olive oil: Extra virgin for cooking and dressings
  • Fatty fish: Salmon, mackerel, sardines (omega-3s)
  • Nut butters: Natural almond or peanut butter

Important: While healthy fats are beneficial, start with smaller portions as high-fat foods can trigger nausea in some people, especially when starting treatment.

Foods to Prioritise

Best Foods for GLP-1 Success

Focus on these nutrient-dense options that are generally well-tolerated:

Vegetables (Unlimited)

  • Leafy greens: spinach, kale, rocket, lettuce
  • Cruciferous: broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts
  • Colourful options: tomatoes, peppers, courgettes, aubergines
  • Root vegetables: sweet potatoes, carrots, beetroot

Lean Proteins

  • Skinless chicken and turkey
  • White fish and oily fish
  • Eggs (prepared without excess fat)
  • Greek yoghurt and cottage cheese
  • Legumes and pulses

Whole Grains (Moderate Portions)

  • Oats and porridge
  • Quinoa and brown rice
  • Whole wheat bread and pasta
  • Barley and farro

Fruits (2-3 Servings Daily)

  • Berries: strawberries, blueberries, raspberries
  • Citrus: oranges, grapefruit, clementines
  • Apples and pears
  • Melon and kiwi

Foods to Limit or Avoid

Certain foods can worsen side effects or work against your weight loss goals:

Limit These Foods:

  • Added sugars: Fizzy drinks, juice, sweets, biscuits, cakes
  • Refined carbohydrates: White bread, white rice, crackers, pastries
  • High-fat fried foods: Chips, fried chicken, doughnuts
  • Spicy foods: Can trigger or worsen nausea and digestive discomfort
  • Alcohol: Can worsen nausea and add empty calories
  • Carbonated drinks: May increase bloating and discomfort
  • Processed meats: Sausages, bacon, deli meats (high in sodium and fat)

Trigger Foods:

Certain foods contribute more to GI side effects. Spicy foods, high-fat foods, greasy meals, and alcohol are common triggers. Keep a food journal to identify your personal triggers.

Essential Eating Tips for GLP-1 Success

1. Stay Hydrated

GLP-1 medications can dial back your sense of thirst, making dehydration a risk. Staying hydrated helps mitigate constipation and nausea.

  • Aim for 8-10 glasses of water or sugar-free beverages daily
  • Keep a water bottle with you at all times
  • Herbal teas and infused water count towards your intake
  • Monitor urine colour (pale yellow indicates good hydration)
  • Track your daily water intake with the GLPer app's hydration logging to ensure you're meeting your hydration goals

2. Eat Smaller, Frequent Meals

  • Aim for 4-6 smaller meals instead of 3 large ones
  • Listen to your body's fullness signals and stop eating when satisfied
  • Don't force yourself to finish portions
  • Use smaller plates to help with portion control

3. Choose Easy-to-Digest Options

Especially when starting treatment or increasing doses, opt for foods that are gentler on your digestive system:

  • Soft, blended, or even liquid meals are often better tolerated
  • Smoothies with protein powder, fruits, and vegetables
  • Soups and broths with vegetables and lean protein
  • Porridge with berries and nuts
  • Scrambled eggs with avocado

4. Plan Protein First

  • Start each meal with your protein source
  • This ensures you get adequate protein even if you can't finish your meal
  • Protein helps preserve muscle mass during weight loss
  • Keeps you feeling satisfied between meals
  • Track your daily protein intake with the GLPer app's food diary to ensure you're meeting your targets

5. Be Mindful of Meal Timing

  • Don't lie down immediately after eating (wait 2-3 hours)
  • Avoid eating large meals close to bedtime
  • Some people find eating their largest meal at lunch helps reduce evening nausea
  • Time your injection consistently with your meal schedule

Working with a Nutritionist

A registered dietitian can help you form an eating plan that's unique to your needs. They can:

  • Monitor your nutritional progress and adjust as needed
  • Ensure you're getting adequate protein, calories, vitamins, minerals, and fluids
  • Help with meal planning to maximise nutrition in smaller portions
  • Identify and address any nutritional deficiencies
  • Create strategies to manage side effects through diet

Remember:

GLP-1 medications are a tool to help you succeed, but nutrition is the foundation. Focus on whole, minimally processed foods, prioritise protein and fibre, stay hydrated, and listen to your body. The habits you build now will support your long-term success.
